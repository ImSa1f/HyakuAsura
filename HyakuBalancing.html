Combat & Skill Balance

Latest balance pass — everything now lives in the new combat module · 80 skills

New systems Rebalanced Bug fixes Buffs Nerfs

What's new this pass NEW

Quick rundown. The rest of the page goes into the actual numbers.

Combat stuff

  • Damage decay — hit someone too many times in a row and your damage goes down. Resets if you stop hitting them.
  • Stun decay when ganged up on — getting jumped by 3+ people no longer locks you in stun forever.
  • Missed skill endlag — whiff a skill and you eat a bit of recovery time before you can act again.
  • Lingering hitboxes are back — skills connect through the full swing now, not just one frame.
  • M1 cooldown shorter by 0.5s — base M1 just feels snappier.
  • M2 doesn't reset M1 combo — you can throw an M2 mid-string and keep your M1 stage.
  • Global extender cooldown of 4s — can't chain three extenders back to back anymore.
  • Wall hit gives you a tiny iframe — 0.6s of free invuln right after you get wall-bounced so you don't immediately get re-comboed.
  • Can't dash and block at the same time — pick one.

Stat / training stuff

  • Stat cap raised to 100k for durability. Fat cap up to 2.5k.
  • Training is slower overall — was way too fast before.
  • But not at 6k–10k — you can still grind that range, just slower than early game.
  • Past 10k is harder, past 20k is real slow, past 50k is endgame whale stuff.
  • Durability actually matters now — going from 3k to 5k dura makes a real difference.
  • Stamina drains are now % of max stamina — high-stam players actually feel drain instead of running forever.
  • Muscle players are slower (sprint + attack speed) but they hit harder. Not useless, just a tradeoff.

Code / structure (dev-side)

  • All combat math lives in one module now (CombatCalculation).
  • All skill metadata lives in one data file (CombatCalcsData) — no more duplicating values across skill modules.

Combat changes

Every gameplay-side combat change with the actual numbers.

Damage decay NEW

If you keep hitting the same person, your damage goes down. Counts both M1s and skills — every hit adds to the count.

  • First 9 hits: full damage.
  • After that, every 9 hits in a row makes your damage drop by another 8%.
  • Won't drop below 50% — combos still work, just less rewarding the longer they go.
  • Tracked per attacker → target. If two people punch the same victim, they each have their own decay count.
  • Resets when you stop hitting them.

Multi-attacker stun decay NEW

Stun gets cut shorter the more people hitting you at once.

People hitting youStun is
1normal
21.5× shorter
33× shorter
4+scales up, capped so it doesn't go infinite

Missing a skill = endlag NEW

If you throw a skill and don't hit anyone, you get a short recovery window before you can do anything else. No more risk-free spam.

Lingering hitbox RESTORED

Skill hitboxes are active for the full swing window again. Late dodges get clipped like they used to.

M1 cooldown shorter BUFF

Base M1 cooldown reduced by 0.5 seconds. M1s feel quicker.

M2 doesn't reset M1 combo CHANGED

Throwing an M2 used to reset your M1 stage cooldown — that's gone. You can weave M2s in mid-combo and pick up where you left off.

Global extender cooldown NERF

All extenders share a single 4-second cooldown. You can use one extender, but you can't chain three different extenders in a row.

Wall hit iframe NEW

When you get launched into a wall, you get 0.6 seconds of iframes right after the impact. Always, no matter what hit you. Stops you from getting wall-stuck and chain-comboed.

Can't dash + block NEW

If you're blocking, you can't dash. If you're mid-dash, you can't block. You have to pick.

How damage scales REBALANCED

Every M1, M2, and skill runs through the same math now.

Damage bonuses that always apply

  • M1 hits get a flat 1.25× bonus.
  • M2 hits get a flat 1.5× bonus.
  • Lean bonus — if your muscle is higher than your fat, you get up to a 3× bonus. Calculated from muscle-to-fat ratio. Slim & muscled hits harder than fat & muscled.

Stamina drain REWORKED

Everything's a percent of your max stamina now. Used to be flat numbers, which meant 100k-stam players barely drained anything. Fixed.

M1 drain

  • Base drain: 2% of your max stamina per swing.
  • More muscle = more drain (up to +2% extra).
  • More strength = more drain (up to +1.5%).
  • More fat = more drain (up to +1.6%).
  • If you land the M1 you only pay about half. Miss it and you pay full.

Running / sprinting drain

  • Base: 1% of max stamina per second.
  • Bigger body (muscle + fat) drains faster, capped so it doesn't go nuts.
  • Tiny extra drain for very high agility.

Build cheat sheet

Quick "what does this build feel like" summary.

Lean / Agility

Hit-and-run skirmisher
  • Fastest sprint
  • Lowest stamina drain
  • Fastest M1 / M2 swings
  • Long sprint duration
  • Lowest damage per hit
  • Squishy

Muscle

Heavy hitter
  • Highest damage per hit (M1 + M2 + lean bonus stack)
  • Tanky HP from muscle
  • ~14% slower sprint
  • ~17–28% slower swings
  • Drains more stamina per swing

Strength

Balanced striker
  • Strong M1 / M2 multiplier
  • Normal sprint speed
  • Normal stamina drain
  • No lean bonus though
  • Average HP

Durability

Tank
  • Eats damage like nothing
  • Double HP pool
  • Way less stamina lost when blocking
  • Doesn't hit harder by itself
  • Best as a hybrid with Muscle or Strength

Directional dodge (player)

Lives in StarterCharacterScripts/MainScript/CRnInput.server.luau · the function is NewDodge.

Distance below is the horizontal body velocity applied every render step for 0.25s. Fat doesn't matter here — only total muscle.

Distance = min(175,
  (58 / (1 + 0.2 * DashCount))
  * (1 + min(Agility, 2000) / 6600)
  * (1 + MuscleBonus))

MuscleBonusFull = min(TM / 1900, 0.8)
MuscleBonus = MuscleBonusFull
  — halved when TM >= 4000 AND DashCount >= 2

Cooldown: 2s per direction · 3s when Muscle >= 500.
