Skill Damage — Buffed
Skill Scaling
All Skill Damage Raised — Stat Divisors Lowered
Skills scaled far weaker than M1/M2 per stat point — a skill on cooldown could hit for less than a free M1. The Strength, Muscle and Fat divisors in the skill formula were lowered so every skill that runs through the standard skill calculation hits harder. Bigger gain the more you invest: roughly +21% at low stats, +30% at high stats.

Durability granted an enormous HP pool — 8,000 Durability gave over 20,000 HP, making high-dura builds take 70+ hits to kill. Every tier of the Durability→Health curve was reduced by roughly 40%. Dura builds are still the tankiest in the game, just no longer unkillable.

4k+ mult1.4×→0.85×

Damage Rework — M1, M2 & Skills
The problem — “M2 felt weaker the more Strength I had”
M1 and M2 used to scale completely differently. As you trained Strength, M1 damage shot up fast while M2 barely moved — so M2 ended up feeling weaker the more you invested, sometimes dropping to around half of your M1 damage. Both attacks now use the same scaling, so this can’t happen anymore.
ChangeDetail
M1 and M2 now scale togetherBoth attacks use the same stat scaling. M2 now lands at a steady ~4× your M1 damage at every Strength level, and both keep climbing as you train — no more shrinking M2.
Punch damage uses Strength, Muscle and Fat onlyAgility and Attack Speed no longer add to M1 or M2 damage — only the three core stats do.
Old “lean” bonus removedThe hidden lean bonus is gone. Before, gaining Fat could actually lower your damage — a fat-heavy build could lose nearly half its punch damage. Now training any stat only ever helps.
Skill damage simplifiedSkill damage also comes purely from Strength, Muscle and Fat now, with no hidden lean penalty.
Balancer note — the M2 damage bonus is currently set high (4×). Now that M1 and M2 scale together, it can be dialed back if M2 ends up hitting too hard.
Skill & Knockback Fixes
FixDetail
Hadouken — huge damage bugHadouken could hit the same target many times in a single cast, spiking its damage to around 970. It now hits each target once for its proper damage, and travels in the direction you actually aimed.
Knockback direction fixSome knockbacks could fling a target in a random direction — occasionally off the map. Knockback is now always aimed properly from the attacker toward the target.

Damage Formulas

How M1, M2 and skill damage are calculated. All three scale off Strength, Muscle and Fat only — Agility and Attack Speed do not affect damage.

How a hit is calculated
M1 punch  =  base damage  ×  stat multiplier  ×  1.25
M2 punch  =  base damage  ×  stat multiplier  ×  4
Skill     =  base power   ×  stat multiplier

Every move has a base value. That gets multiplied by your stat multiplier, then by a flat bonus for the attack type. From the same stats, an M2 lands roughly 4× harder than an M1.

Your stat multiplier

Your stat multiplier grows with Strength, Muscle and Fat — the more you have, the bigger your hits. Every style and skill leans on those three stats differently; the exact weightings are in the Style Scaling and Skill Scaling tables further down. Stats have diminishing returns (see Effective Stats below), and punches reward raw stats slightly harder than skills do.

Flat multipliers
MultiplierValue
M1 bonus1.25×
M2 bonus4.0×
Base skill multiplier floor0.75×
M1 and M2 scale the same. They use the same stat multiplier, so M2 always stays a steady amount above M1 — train your stats and both go up together. Skills use a slightly gentler curve, but their higher base power means they still hit hardest per use.

Combat Rules
Combo limits
  • One ragdoll move per combo string — the first ragdoll move lands, the rest are blocked until the combo resets.
  • Maximum 2 extenders per string — the third will not connect.
  • Extender / grab damage drop — each connecting extender or grab makes following hits deal 20% less (stacking).
  • Landing an M1 halves recovery — whiffing pays full endlag.
  • Walking while punching is 18% slower.

Blocking & Perfect Block
Attack typeParry window
M1 (regular punch)0.2s
M2 / Critical0.5s
Skills0.5s
  • Can’t block for 0.3s after whiffing a skill.
  • Perfect Block is automatic — starting a block opens a brief parry window. Time your block just before a hit lands and it becomes a Perfect Block. No key combo needed.
  • 2s cooldown on the Perfect Block window — re-tapping block won’t refresh the window instantly. Normal blocking is never gated by this.
  • You can dash while blocking — but you can’t start a block mid-dash (during the dash animation).
Burst bar
  • Every hit taken — including grabs and 0-damage contacts — fills Burst by at least 5 of 100.
  • Damage-dealing hits fill more, scaled to HP lost.
  • At 100 Burst you can break out of a combo and stun nearby opponents.
Rhythm bar
  • Damage — up to +15% at full Rhythm.
  • Stamina — up to −30% on all combat stamina costs at full Rhythm.
  • Builds by landing hits, decays when you stop fighting.
